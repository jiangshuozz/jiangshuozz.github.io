Yiyin Wang received the B.S. degree in electrical engineering from Fudan University, Shanghai, China, in 2002, the M.S. degree (cum laude) in microelectronics from Delft University of Technology (TU Delft), The Netherlands, and Fudan University, China, in 2005, respectively, and the Ph.D. degree in electrical engineering from TU Delft, The Netherlands, in 2011. She is currently an Associate Professor at the center of Intelligent Wireless Networking and Cooperative Control (I-WiN-C2) with the Department of Automation, Shanghai Jiao Tong University, China. Prior to that, she was a Research Assistant at the Circuits and Systems group (CAS), TU Delft from 2006 to 2007. From February 2010 till July 2010, she visited Georgia Institute of Technology (Gatech), Atlanta, USA. She was a Postdoc Fellow at TU Delft and then Gatech from 2011 to 2013.

Her research interests lie in the general area of signal processing for communications and networking (SPCOM). To be more specific, she currently focuses on synchronization and localization for terrestrial and underwater wireless sensor networks, and underwater communications.
